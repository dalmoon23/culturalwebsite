TRADITIONAL ATTIRES IN MELANAU MUKAH

Melanau Traditional Attires (Wedding)

The Melanau group places a lot of emphasis on rank in their marriage traditions. To preserve their dignity and heritage, social position is heavily emphasized. In order to maintain the social standing of the Melanau marriage, the bride and groom are required to bring stuff and gifts as a token of their engagement and dowry. The Melanau community refers to couples with the same social standing as "sama gara," whereas a male and female relationship with a different social standing is referred to as "sidat adat." Dark satin is used for male attire in Melanau, which is paired with songket material that is covered in flowers and sengkulon headwear from Java. Women from the Melanau culture typically wear a black velvet dress with a Sarawak-woven Brunei songket sarong, a big hanging belt, dokoh, and kepih bun head accessories.

Melanau Traditional Attires (Women)

There are two forms of traditional clothing for Melanau women: velvet clothing and Baban clothing. Melanau women dress in a black velvet shirt, a Brunei songket sarong made of Sarawak woven fabric, and a big hanging belt that is adorned with dokoh and kepih bun head ornaments. Baban clothing, the ancient Melanau wedding attire, is styled by women when they wear these clothes. A socket cloth is coupled with a short black kurung shirt. Wear a kepih chest, kepih bun, and two pieces of red scarf on your chest.

Melanau Traditional Attires (Men)

The clothing worn by the Melanau people in the Sarawak region is quite unique and interesting to them. The traditional attire worn by Melanau men is different from what is worn by women. In this instance, men will wear a hat, a modern cut shirt, and golden orange brocade tied around their waists. Dark satin is used in the men's clothes worn by Melanau, together with songket cloth decorated with flowers and sengkulon headwear from the Jawa.
