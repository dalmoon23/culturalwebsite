INTERESTING EVENT IN MUKAH, SARAWAK

PESTA KAUL

Every Melanau person in Sarawak is required to participate in the Pesta Kaul, a celebration or event, which is held once a year. The Pesta Kaul is a festival that displays the unique identities of each of Sarawak's Melanau cultures. The day of the Kaul celebration has come as a representation of identity for any Melanau person as well as ethnic groups from Sarawak such as the Bidayuh, Kenyah, Kayan, and others. The celebration represents the coming together of the race. Surprisingly every Mukah resident takes advantage of the chance to host a miniature Kaul in their local village with a special theme while upholding the Kaul tradition. In the tea village, where there are more than 100 homes, each house's representative, Mukah, must offer a meal.

PESTA BEDERUN

Perhaps many individuals don't know what "BEDERUN" is, even though we are aware of the history of the event, which has a very unusual name. The Balingian festival known as Bederun, which in the Melanau language of Balingian means GATHERING, is held every two years. The Bederun Festival's organizers have their own goal, which is to further strengthen relationships between Balingians and people from other regions. There are a huge number of events taking place during this festival, including a decorated raft competition and a net boat race in addition to the Bidar Boat Rowing Competition in the categories of 8 paddlers, 15 paddlers, and full capacity. This time, the Bederun Festival was further energized by the presence of a Cultural Night Programme and the addition of community and sales booths.
